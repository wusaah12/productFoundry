# Product Foundry

## Overview

Product Foundry is a structured discovery framework that runs **before the first sprint**. It takes a raw product idea through seven gated stages and produces specification-complete features ready for delivery.

**Core Philosophy:** Build the right thing before building anything.

## What It Does

Product Foundry fills the gap between Agile's speed and Waterfall's rigor. It validates product direction before development begins, ensuring teams build features grounded in evidence, not assumptions.

### The 7-Stage Process

```
Stage 1: Idea Brief → Stage 2: Discovery → Stage 3: Hypothesis → Stage 4: Vision & Mission
  → Stage 5: Roadmap → Stage 6: Release Plan → Stage 7: Feature Specs
```

Each stage produces a locked artifact. The next stage cannot open until the previous one is locked. No skipping.

## Key Principles

1. **Process over preference** — Gates are never relaxed for convenience
2. **Human judgment is sovereign** — AI surfaces options; humans make decisions
3. **Transparency over convenience** — Problems are named when they exist
4. **Vision as the highest filter** — Once locked, Vision tests every downstream artifact
5. **Roles at the right moment** — Specialists join only at stages where their perspective changes output
6. **Testability as the standard of done** — Feature specs are complete only when every acceptance criterion is independently verifiable

## Artifacts

Each stage produces a specific artifact:

- **Stage 1:** Idea Brief (problem, user, workaround, urgency, role)
- **Stage 2:** Discovery Report (user research, competitive analysis, market validation)
- **Stage 3:** Hypothesis (falsifiable hypothesis with SMART metrics)
- **Stage 4:** Vision & Mission (strategic direction, success definition)
- **Stage 5:** Product Roadmap (sequenced features, prioritization rationale)
- **Stage 6:** Release Plan (MVP scope, phasing, dependencies)
- **Stage 7:** Feature Specs (BDD scenarios, acceptance criteria, user stories)

## Target Users

- Product teams building new products or major features
- Organizations wanting to validate ideas before investing in development
- Teams using Agile but lacking structured discovery
- Anyone who wants to avoid building the wrong thing

## Success Criteria

A Product Foundry session is successful when:
- The problem is validated with evidence
- The vision is locked and unambiguous
- Feature specs are testable and require no clarification
- The delivery team can act on specs without asking questions
