## Interview Overview

**Goal**: Understand how users currently solve this problem and what frustrates them  
**Duration**: 30 minutes (keep it tight; respect their time)  
**Interview Type**: Semi-structured (ask core questions; follow threads)  
**Tone**: Curious, not leading (Listen more than you talk)

## Interview Script

### Warm-Up (5 minutes)

**Goal**: Build rapport, set context, get permission to record/take notes

**Your opening**:
```
"Thanks for taking time today. I'm working on a product to help with [problem from Idea Brief].
I'd like to understand your current workflow and what frustrates you.
There are no right answers—I'm just curious about your real experience.
Is it OK if I take notes? [If recording: Is it OK if I record this so I can capture everything accurately?]
Great. Let's dive in."
```

### Core Discovery Questions (15 minutes)

**Ask these questions in order, but let them talk.** If they mention something interesting, follow it. Come back to the guide after they finish.

**Q1: Current Workflow**
```
"Walk me through how you currently handle [problem]. Start from the beginning.
Take your time—I want to understand every step."
```

*Why this works*: Gets them to describe workflow in their own words. Listen for:
- Actual steps (not what they think they do)
- Pain points (where they complain)
- Time investment (how long it takes)
- Who's involved (other people in the workflow)
- Tools used today (email, spreadsheet, software)

**Notes**:
- [Jot down key steps they mention]
- [What did they complain about?]
- [How long does this take?]

---

**Q2: The Biggest Pain Point**
```
"Of everything you just described, what's the most frustrating part? Why?"
```

*Why this works*: Identifies the single biggest problem. Listen for:
- The specific step that wastes the most time
- The step with the most errors
- The step that requires back-and-forth with others
- The emotional pain ("I hate when...")

**Notes**:
- [What's their top frustration?]
- [Why is it frustrating? (time, quality, effort, emotion)]
- [How often does this happen?]

---

**Q3: Impact of the Problem**
```
"How does [biggest pain point] affect your work? What's the consequence?"
```

*Why this works*: Quantifies impact. Listen for:
- Time wasted (hours per week/month)
- Errors or rework
- Stress or frustration
- Impact on others (downstream delays)
- Cost or compliance issues

**Notes**:
- [How much time does this waste?]
- [Does it create errors?]
- [Who else is affected?]

---

**Q4: Current Workarounds**
```
"How do you work around this problem today? What's your workaround?"
```

*Why this works*: Shows how much users care (they're already trying to fix it). Listen for:
- Hacks or manual steps
- Tools they use to compensate
- What they wish existed

**Notes**:
- [What workaround do they use?]
- [Is it effective? Partially?]
- [What would ideal look like?]

---

**Q5: Must-Have vs. Nice-to-Have**
```
"If we fixed [biggest pain point], what would change for you?
What would you be able to do that you can't do now?"
```

*Why this works*: Surfaces outcomes they care about. Listen for:
- Time they'd save (e.g., "I'd get 3 hours back per week")
- Quality improvements (e.g., "Fewer errors")
- New capabilities (e.g., "I could finally see what's pending")
- Downstream benefits (e.g., "Finance would process faster")

**Notes**:
- [What's the benefit if we fixed this?]
- [How much is this worth to them?]
- [Would they pay for a solution?]

---

**Q6: Constraints or Blockers**
```
"Are there any constraints I should know about? For example, tools you have to use,
compliance requirements, security concerns, or technical limitations?"
```

*Why this works*: Surfaces "showstoppers" early. Listen for:
- Required integrations (e.g., "Must work with QuickBooks")
- Compliance/security (e.g., "Financial data must be encrypted")
- Technical debt (e.g., "Our system is on-premise only")
- Organizational constraints (e.g., "We can't install new software")

**Notes**:
- [What systems must it work with?]
- [Any compliance/security requirements?]
- [Any show-stoppers?]

---

**Q7: Current Usage Patterns**
```
"Where do you do this work? On your laptop? Mobile? Combination?
When do you typically do this? During business hours? Anytime?"
```

*Why this works*: Surfaces device/location/timing constraints. Listen for:
- Mobile vs. desktop (% of time on each)
- Location (office, traveling, home)
- Timing (9-5, weekends, ad-hoc, batched)

**Notes**:
- [When and where do they work on this?]
- [What device do they use?]
- [Is mobile required or optional?]

---

### Workflow Walkthrough (10 minutes)

**If appropriate, ask to see them do it**:
```
"Would you be willing to walk me through a real example? Like, open up your email
or your system and show me exactly what you do? I want to see the actual clicks."
```

*Why this works*: Reveals steps they forgot to mention or underestimate time. Observe:
- Exact steps (what they actually click)
- Time to complete
- Errors or confusion (where they struggle)
- Tools used (what software appears)

**Notes**:
- [What did we learn from watching them do it?]
- [Did they mention any steps they forgot to say?]
- [How long did it actually take?]
- [Where did they struggle?]

---

### Closing (5 minutes)

**Q8: Would They Use It?**
```
"If a product existed that [solved biggest pain point],
would you use it? Why or why not?"
```

*Why this works*: Tests basic interest. Listen for:
- Hesitation (reason to investigate deeper)
- Enthusiasm (signal of strong need)
- Conditions (e.g., "Only if it works with X")

**Notes**:
- [Would they actually use this?]
- [What conditions would make it essential?]

---

**Q9: Who Else Should We Talk To?**
```
"Who else should I talk to understand this problem?
Are there other roles affected by this process?
(e.g., their manager, teammates, the person who receives their output)"
```

*Why this works*: Identifies secondary personas and next interviews. Listen for:
- Manager/approver perspective
- Downstream person (who receives this person's output)
- Similar-role person (to validate if this is universal)

**Notes**:
- [Who else should we interview?]
- [What's their contact info? Can you introduce us?]

---

**Your closing**:
```
"Thanks so much for your time. This was really helpful.
If we build something to solve [problem], would you be willing to test it?
[Get contact info if interested in beta testing]
"
```

## Interview Variations

### If interviewing a MANAGER (not front-line user):

Add questions about:
- How do you know your team has problems with X?
- What metrics do you track?
- What's your biggest operational pain?
- How much time do your team waste on X per week?

### If interviewing an ACCOUNTANT (downstream person):

Add questions about:
- What data issues come from upstream?
- What would make your job easier?
- What errors are you catching and fixing?

### If interviewing a TECHNICAL PERSON (engineer, admin):

Add questions about:
- What systems/APIs must we integrate with?
- What compliance requirements?
- What technical constraints?

## Tips for Good Interviews

✅ **DO**:
- Listen more than you talk
- Ask "why" 2-3 times (get to root cause)
- Let silence happen (they'll fill it with good info)
- Ask permission before recording
- Take notes
- Be genuinely curious

❌ **DON'T**:
- Lead the witness ("You must hate when...")
- Talk about your product ("We're building a system that...")
- Argue with their feedback ("But actually, you could just...")
- Interview only people like you (talk to people with different roles/experiences)
- Skip the warm-up (rapport matters)
